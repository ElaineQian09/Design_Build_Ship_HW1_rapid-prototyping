Neighborhoods Worth Eating Through

Every block has a story. These are the ones told best at the table.

Pilsen

Southwest Side

Taquerias on every corner, murals on every wall. Pilsen's food scene is as vivid as its streetscape.

Chinatown

South Side

Dim sum carts, hand-pulled noodles, and bakeries that have been here longer than most skyscrapers.

Logan Square

Northwest Side

Where chef-driven restaurants meet late-night elote carts. Ambitious and unpretentious at once.

Hyde Park

South Side

University energy, lakefront calm, and comfort food institutions that have served generations.

Uptown

North Side

Vietnamese pho houses, Ethiopian injera spots, and a quiet diversity that rewards the curious.

Little Italy

Near West Side

Red sauce, cold cuts, and espresso — the old neighborhood holds its ground with pride.

The Icons

Three dishes that every visitor argues about and every local defends.

🍕

Deep-Dish Pizza

Buttery crust, layers of mozzarella, chunky tomato on top. Built like architecture, eaten like a commitment.

🥪

Italian Beef

Thin-sliced roast beef on Italian bread, dipped in jus with giardiniera. Napkins are mandatory.

🌭

Chicago-Style Hot Dog

All-beef frank, poppy seed bun, seven toppings, zero ketchup. A city's identity in one bite.

Why Taste Chicago

"You can learn more about a city in one meal at a neighborhood counter than in a week of sightseeing."

— The idea behind this project
